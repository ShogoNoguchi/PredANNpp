Expectation and Acoustic Neural Network Representations Enhance Music Identification from Brain Activity

PredANN++ pretrains an EEG encoder by predicting discretized teacher representations from audio: Acoustic (MuQ) and Expectation (MusicGen-derived Surprisal / Entropy), using multitask learning with 50% masking. The learned representations improve EEG-based Song ID classification and provide complementary gains when ensembled.

Task: 10-way Song ID from EEG (NMED-T)
Input: 3s EEG (128ch @125Hz)
Teachers: MuQ / Surprisal / Entropy
Eval: SW_valid deterministic cropping

Main Claim. EEG representation learning should be guided by neurobiologically distinct teacher representations (acoustic vs expectation), not merely random initialization diversity.

Overview (Abstract)

During music listening, cortical activity encodes both acoustic and expectation-related information. Prior work has shown that ANN representations resemble cortical representations and can serve as supervisory signals for EEG recognition. Here we show that distinguishing acoustic and expectation-related ANN representations as teacher targets improves EEG-based music identification. Models pretrained to predict either representation outperform non-pretrained baselines, and combining them yields complementary gains that exceed strong seed ensembles formed by varying random initializations. These findings show that teacher representation type shapes downstream performance and that representation learning can be guided by neural encoding.

Demo Track Surprisal/Entropy Visualization

Visualization for Surprisal/Entropy Q128 sequences. The page loads three demo tracks and synchronized Surprisal/Entropy data (context window of 16 seconds). Timeline sync is aligned to segment_start_s, with a hard upper bound of 240 sec (shorter tracks end at their natural duration).

Discretization method: continuous Surprisal and Entropy sequences were pooled across all songs (NMED-T 10 songs + 3 demo tracks), then feature-wise 128-bin equal-frequency quantile binning was applied. Each frame value was converted to a discrete label in the range 0–127 using the resulting bin boundaries.
